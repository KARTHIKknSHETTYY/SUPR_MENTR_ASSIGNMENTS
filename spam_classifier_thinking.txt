Description: Design a spam detection system by identifying useful features, required data, and possible mistakes.


1. Features for Spam Detection

Features are the characteristics the model uses to decide whether a message is spam or not.

Keywords in message: Words like “free”, “win”, “offer”, “lottery”, “urgent”.

Number of links: Spam messages often contain many links.

Sender information: Unknown or suspicious email addresses.

Message length: Spam messages may be unusually short or very long.

Special characters or symbols: Excessive use of symbols like “!!!”, “$$$”, or capital letters.


2. Data Needed

To train the spam detection system, we need a labeled dataset.

Spam messages: Emails or SMS that are already identified as spam.

Normal messages: Genuine messages that are not spam.

Message text: Content of the email or SMS.

Sender details: Information about who sent the message.

Labels: Each message marked as “spam” or “not spam”.



3. Possible Mistakes in the System

False Positive: A normal message is incorrectly classified as spam.
Example: A genuine promotional email from a store marked as spam.

False Negative: A spam message is incorrectly classified as normal.
Example: A scam email reaching the inbox.

Changing spam patterns: Spammers constantly change words and formats to bypass filters.

Data bias: If the training data is limited or unbalanced, the model may not detect all types of spam.
